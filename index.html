About

Who I am.

As an incoming M.S. in Data Science student at Columbia University, I aim to build a career at the intersection of machine learning, sustainability, and social good. My experience in ML research at Mount Sinai School of Medicine has taught me that rigorous, human-centered data science — particularly when applied to complex clinical populations — requires both technical depth and ethical intentionality. Working on NLP-driven psychiatric research showed me how AI can surface patterns in human behavior that genuinely inform care, and deepened my commitment to deploying models responsibly.

My current work as an ML Engineer at CropGuardian has taken that conviction into the world of climate tech. Building Sentinel-2 satellite imagery pipelines for crop health monitoring and deforestation detection has shown me how machine learning, when applied to Earth observation data, can become a real tool for environmental accountability — not just a technical exercise.

I am about to receive my B.A. in Data Science from New York University's Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences, with a minor in Chemistry, where I will receive research honors for my work at Mount Sinai. I plan to bring my experience and passion to Columbia University's M.S. in Data Science program this fall.

Outside of engineering, I am passionate about fostering cats, traveling, and having a creative outlet.
